# Risk Disclosure

This MIT-licensed experimental dry-run preview can read data from an
event-contract exchange but permanently blocks order submission. It can still
expose sensitive account data or produce incorrect simulations. It is an
engineering and educational project, not individualized financial,
investment, legal, tax, or trading advice.

## Material risks

- **Market risk:** contract prices and settlement outcomes can move against you.
- **Execution risk:** orders can be rejected, delayed, partially filled, filled at an unexpected time, or remain in a queue.
- **Liquidity risk:** a displayed price does not guarantee executable depth or an exit.
- **Fee risk:** fees and fee-rule changes can erase a small apparent spread.
- **Automation risk:** defects, stale state, race conditions, API changes, clock drift, duplicate processes, and unexpected data can cause unintended behavior.
- **Availability risk:** exchange, network, DNS, TLS, WebSocket, operating-system, or power failures can interrupt monitoring or order management.
- **Credential risk:** anyone with a valid API Key ID and matching private key may be able to act on the associated account within granted permissions.
- **Model and assumption risk:** historical, demo, simulated, or backtested behavior may not represent live performance.
- **Regulatory and eligibility risk:** availability, rules, taxes, and legal treatment vary by person and jurisdiction and can change.

## No performance promise

No return, win rate, fill rate, fee level, queue position, settlement value, uptime, hedge coverage, or loss limit is guaranteed. The words “profit,” “edge,” “opportunity,” “guard,” “safe,” and “protective” describe intended logic or controls—not assured outcomes.

## User responsibilities

You are responsible for understanding the code, confirming account eligibility and permissions, reviewing current official exchange documentation, protecting credentials and account output, maintaining backups, and stopping the preview when evidence is unclear. Do not modify this copy to trade; use the separately reviewed 10x1c flagship for any order-capable workflow.

Never trade money needed for living expenses, debt payments, taxes, emergency savings, or other essential obligations. Consider independent professional advice appropriate to your circumstances.
